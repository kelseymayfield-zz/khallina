1. عمـ + بـ + فعل مضارع

Like several other Arabic dialects, Levantine dialect adds the preposition بـ to present-tense verbs. The only difference is the addition of عمـ before بـ, which usually implies that the action is being done at the moment of speaking. The عمـ + بـ + فعل مضارع is also used in the past tense after كان, for example:
هو يشتغل بالبانك = He works at the bank
هو عمبيشتغل بالبانك = He is working at the bank right now
هو كان عمبيشتغل بالبانك = He was working at the bank

2. بَد + ضمير

In Levantine Arabic, we use بَد with a possessive pronoun in order to express want, wish, or desire. The word بد is followed by a possessive pronoun suffix to denote who the subject is.

أنابد + ي = بَدّيهوبد + ه = بَدّْه
أنتبد + ك = بدُكهيبد + ها = بَدّها
أنتبد + ك = بَدّكهمبد + هُن = بَدّهُن
أنتمبد + كْن = بَدّكْننحنبد + نا = بَدنا
